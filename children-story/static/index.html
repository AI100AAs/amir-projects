How it works

  1. You type a topic. Any idea becomes a story.
  2. The AI writes a story and draws an illustration. A language model running on your Mac creates a short, age-appropriate story. An illustration is generated automatically.
  3. You ask for changes. Not happy with something? Tell the AI what to change and it rewrites the story.
  4. You record your voice. Read a sentence for 5-10 seconds. The AI learns what your voice sounds like.
  5. The AI reads the story aloud. Using voice cloning (XTTS-v2), it creates a narration that sounds like your voice.
  6. You listen and enjoy. Play the story, download it, or save it for later.

The AI behind StoryWhisper

Story writing

Large Language Model

A language model (like google/gemma-4-e4b) generates and revises the story from your topic and feedback. It runs locally on your Mac through LM Studio, with text streaming in live as it writes.

Illustration

Bonsai MLX

A compact image generation model running on Apple Silicon MLX creates a watercolor-style illustration from the story. Generated automatically after each story.

Voice cloning

XTTS-v2 (Coqui)

A zero-shot voice cloning model that learns your voice from a short sample and synthesizes speech that sounds like you. It runs locally using PyTorch on your Mac's CPU.

Backup narration

Neural Text-to-Speech

If you prefer not to clone your voice, edge-tts provides high-quality neural voices that read the story in a tone matching your pitch range.

Safety

Consent & disclosure

Voice cloning requires explicit consent. The app warns about cloning risks and labels every narration as AI-generated, so no one is fooled.

Privacy

Everything stays local

The language model, image generation, voice cloning, and audio generation all run on your Mac. Your voice sample and stories never leave your computer.
